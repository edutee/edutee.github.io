HOW DID WE GET HERE ?

OUR STORY

EduTee started as a campaign, born from the passion for underrepresented students in Higher Education to have a voice in their education. Whilst the passion has grown beyond Higher Education Institutions, every child, and every learner from all backgrounds must continue to have access to education. That is an education that is equitable, inclusive, accessible and reflects them.


Policies and procedures that cater to enhancements in education are done to students, without them, and this is where we close the gap.


We want to work with learners, schools, Higher Education Institutions and organisations to invest in education, co-create student experiences, and empower the future generation.





WHOSE IDEA IS THIS ?

OUR FOUNDER

Our founder, Theresa Ofure Ogbekhiulu, is a young leader in education and social justice. She is a public speaker and a seasoned workshop facilitator for anti-racism, decolonising in Higher Education and creating inclusive spaces.

Theresa was awarded the Black History Wales Academic achievement award in 2022 and her story was featured on BBC Wales. She was nominated for a young leader award, in recognition of my contribution to the community.

As a School Governor, Political Education officer, Swansea West Labour Party, and Chair, Swansea Young Labour, she understands the importance of getting involved in the wheel of change, and not standing on the sidelines.

With a robust academic background in Wales, comprising a two master’s degrees in an ESRC-accredited MSc in Social Research Methods, and an MA in Communications, Media Practice & Public Relations, both with a distinction and an Undergraduate in BA in Communications and Public Relations, with a first-class all at Swansea University, Theresa is experienced in research and utilising data for advocacy.

Additionally, being the Senior Project Advisor (Race Equality/ EDI) at Swansea University, she is responsible for leading the EDI initiatives across the University and developing an action plan for race equality.

In 2019 and 2020, I was elected Education Officer and trustee at Swansea University’s Students’ Union and the University’s Council, representing the academic interests of over 20,000 students. In these roles, I campaigned for, championed, and supported students from minorities' backgrounds, including students from minority ethnic backgrounds.

In 2019, the Black History Month campaign led by me for Swansea University Student’s Union, won the National Union of Students Wales Liberation Campaign of the Year award.

In her spare time, she enjoys swimming and teaching. She has recently completed a swim assistant teaching qualification with Swim Wales, to encourage and teach more people how to swim through the Black Swimming Association.
